SMART-Seq2 Description

SMART-Seq2 is a single-cell RNA-seq method that generates full transcript length information. It is currently the most widely published single-cell RNA-Seq method. Current limitations include restriction to polyadenylated mRNAs and lack of strand information. We have developed an automated pipeline that begins with 96 well plates containing single-cell lysates and ends with Illumina sequencing data.

Workflow

Our automated workflow is optimised for 4x 96 well plates per run. Fewer plates are possible.

  • Inputs are 96 well plates containing cell lysates
  • Optional stopping point amplified cDNA products
  • Optional stopping point pooled libraries ready for Illumina sequencing
  • Output is a fastq and bam file for each single-cell

Important information

Reliable single-cell FACS sorting is a critical parameter. Please consult your respective FACS core facility before optimizing single-cell sorting. The cell isolation should be as rapid as possible to preserve gene expression programs and viability. Tissue dissociation may require optimization.

We are required to optimize the number of PCR cycles for amplification and concentration of ERCCs (synthetic mRNAs) to spike into the assay. These paramenters are influenced by the RNA content of your cell of interest which is proportional to cell size. Optimization requires a number of test plates. These can as little as 12 cells per well. We will amplify these samples and perform quality control to ensure processing of the real samples is as optimized as possible.

Due to the cost of quality control we cannot guarantee completely even number of sequencing reads per cell. We perform QC on 10% of samples per 96 well plate and use the average value to calculate concentrations for pooling.

The maximum number of cells we can currently multiplex on a lane of Illumina sequencing is 384. To estimate the number of reads per cell divide the number of reads per lane.

Controls

To assist with quality control experimentally in addition to in the analysis proper controls are necessary. You are required to sort at least 1 multi-cell control (10 cells) and 1 empty well control. To minimize batch effects and improve data quaility it is beneficial to have mixed treatments on the same plate eg. half a plate with single-cells from wild-type and half a plate with single-cells from knockout.

Sample submission inputs

  • Project name
  • Project description
  • Cell size
  • Number of 96 well plates
  • Desired number of sequencing reads per cell:
  • Desired sequencing length

Instructions

  1. Complete this sample submission form
  2. We will provide you consumables to collect samples including plates, lysis buffer and plate seals
  3. Upon receipt of the plates centrifuge briefly to collect liquid at the bottom of wells.
  4. During collection of cells by FACS keep on ice during sort then centrifuge again to collect cells at the bottom of the well.
  5. Snap freeze cells by placing in dry ice for several minutes then store in -80 freezer long-term. Ship cells to us on dry ice.
