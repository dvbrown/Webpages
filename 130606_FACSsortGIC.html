Materials

  • Complete media for sample collection
  • FACS buffer. Store this buffer cold
    1. Dulbecco's PBS - 50mL
    2. 1% Fetal Calf Serum (FCS) - 500uL
    3. 2mM EDTA - 200uL of 0.5M
    4. Propidium Iodide - add just before sort
  • 45uM cell strainer
  • FACS tubes
  • Accutase
  • Wash buffer - DMEM/F12 + Pen/ Strep
  • CD133-APC conjugate antibody (AC133 #130-098-829)
  • Isotype control Keyhole limpet hemocyanin (not expressed in humans) BD Bio340442

Procedure for neurosphere dissociation

Work quickly and on ice to maximise viability.

  1. Pipette cells in dishes into 50mL falcon tube. Rinse empty wells with some wash buffer and add to tube.
  2. Spin 500xg for 1 minute in a centrifuge at room temp.
  3. Resuspend in 5mL wash buffer and spin at 500g again.
  4. Resuspend in 500uL - 3mL accutase as appropriate for cell number
  5. Incubate at room temp for 2x 5 minutes, resuspending cells with a pipette in between.
  6. Dilute accutase with 4mL FACS buffer (cold). Put through 45uM cell strainer into a fresh 50mL tube. Pipette some FACS buffer first to wet the membrane
  7. Take 20uL for a trypan blue count and record cell number on sample sheet.
  8. Remember to replate some cells into a single well of 6 well plate.
  9. Spin down at 500xg at 4°C for 5 minutes.
  10. Resuspend in 90uL for 100uL final vol FACS buffer and transfer to a FACS tube.
    Add 5uL (1:21) CD133 antibody or isotype control.
  11. Incubate for 20 minutes in the dark in the fridge (dark).
  12. Wash with 1mL of FACS buffer and centrifuge for 500xg at 4°C for 5 minutes.
  13. Resuspend in a sufficient amount minimum 500uL of FACS buffer for FACS sorting. Aim for 5 - 10 million cells per mL.
  14. Before sorting add 1:100 of 1mg/mL (final 1ug/mL) PI to each tube. This is 5uL of Iwan's buffer for 500uL.

FACS sorting

  1. Use the 100uM nozzle for less pressure through the sorter.
  2. Prepare two (CD133pos and neg) collection tubes filled with 1mL complete media per sample.
  3. Be sure to run the PI, isotype control and CD133 positive control first.

Limiting dilution sphere formation

From a protocol given by Fred Hollande from the ELDA paper by Hu and Symth 2009.

  1. Start with the limiting dilution first, then do the bulk collection
  2. Prepare 2x low attachment 96 well plates (Labelled) with 100uL of media
  3. The sorter can only dispense into one plate at a time
  4. Set the sorter to collect 1, 10, 100 and 1,000 cells in 24-plex ie 2 rows of the plate
  5. Incubate for 5 - 7 days and score for the presence or absence of spheres
  6. Determine neurosphere formation frequency using the ELDA web program from the WEHI bioinformatics website
