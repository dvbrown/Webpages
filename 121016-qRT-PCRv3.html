Quantitative reverse transcription PCR

This method uses SYBR green detection of all double stranded DNA

You will need:

  • Anywhere from 10ng to 1μg total RNA
  • Reverse transcription kit (first strand synthesis)
  • SYBR green mix
  • 384 well plate for Lightcycler
  • 96 well (V-bottom) for samples
  • A plating robot
  • A lightcycler 480 qPCR machine

Reverse transcription

The choice of kit is largely arbitrary. A mixture of random hexamer and poly-dT primer works best.
A pe-incubation of RNA and primers at 65 •C for 10 min enables more efficient priming. Make sure you include a non transcription control.
ie: include all buffers but not the enzyme.

Setting up your 384 well plate map

The plating robot pipettes the SYBR green master mix from 1.5mL tubes first in horizontal fashion. Therefore set up horizontal 384 well replicates with the master mix in replicate.
From experience technical replicates have high accuracy so technical duplicates are recommended. Biological triplicates are always necessary for publication.
The robot then adds the 96 well samples in batches of replicates. What this means is the samples are changing with each block of replicates (eg primers).

Set up of your samples on a 96 well plate

Generally the samples are the primers arrayed in 96 well format.
However they can be the cDNA if you have many samples with only a few targets.

  1. Array your primers horizontally on a 96 well plate
  2. The plating robot can handle replicates but it pipettes from the 96 well plate after SYBR in the 1.5mL tube
  3. Click on the short cut to robotics icon on the desktop
  4. Use a the file DanB_template as a template
  5. Add your samples to the 96 well plate by clicking its image and selecting "new bank".Then select your wells, type in your sample name, add to selection and click "done"
  6. As above for your mastermix in the left most panel, representing 1.5mL tubes. Set to aliquot 6μL.
  7. Now you are ready to set up your 384 well plate. Click on the 384 well image and click "add" on the right panel. From the drop list choose your sample and then the master mix.
    Set the volume dispensed to 4uL and the correct number of replicates (eg 3)
  8. Load 50μL tips in the appropriate positions on the deck. Make sure they click into the receptacle. Right click the tip panel and set to "all available tips".
  9. Press the green arrow to begin the run. You have the option of export the plate map. You will need a USB stick.
  • The machine will stop if it runs out of tips and you will have to load some more. Click on the appropriate postion in the warning screen to restart the run.

    • Set up of your samples on the lightcycler instrument

    Remember to seal the surface of your plate

    1. Click on the lightcycler icon on the desktop
    2. The username is "admin" and password is "LightCycler480". Case sensitive Choose GEL DB R database to log on to
    3. Select the appropriate detection method. I use monocolour hydrolysis probe. Set the plate volume to 10μL
    4. Include 3 steps
      • Preincubation 50 •C for 2 min.
        95 •C for 2 min
      • Amplification 95 •C for 15 sec then 55 - 65 •C for 30 sec
      • A melt curve for SYBR green, continuos measurement
    5. Press the green start button to begin your run

    Export data from the lightcycler

    The Roche software uses the 2nd derivative maximum technique to determine crossing point (threshold). This is the beginning of the log linear portion of the amplification curve.

    1. Click on analysis tab. Go to create new analysis -> absolute quantification and 2nd derivative.
    2. You will see the amplification curves. Now set the analysis to high confidence and then click calculate. This takes a few minutes.
    3. Change to the results panel and export the data as a text file you can open in excel
    4. Click + to generate a new analysis -> Tm calling. Set to SYBR green I format and click calculate to see the melting curves

    Analysis by ddCT method

    The ddCT method is a relative quantification formula that assumes the amplification rate of your templates are equal.

    1. Use the python script 'transposeLinear.py' to convert the 384 well plate map to a linear vector.
    2. In R import the 384wellLayoutTranspose.txt file and bind it to the transpose sample map using cbind
    3. Use the custom annotate sample function to annotate the raw Ct file
    4. Use the split sample name function to split the annotation by whitespace into separate gene and sample factors
    5. In Excel use the ddCt formula 2^((houseKeepingX - targetX) - (houseKeepingC - targetC)). X stands for unknown sample and C for Control sample.
    6. Plot in SPSS using the clustered bar chart
