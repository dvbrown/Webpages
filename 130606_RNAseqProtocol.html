Procedure for production of cDNA libraries for RNA-seq

You will need

  • 50 - 250ng of high quality RNA
  • Actinomycin D. Stock 5ug/uL to 0.1ug/uL.
  • 1x RNA clean-up kit column per sample (eg Macher Nagel XS)
  • 4x DNA clean up column per sample (eg QIAquick)
  • 255uLAMpure XP beads. Bring to room temperature before use
  • Fresh 80% ethanol for use with the beads
  • Unique index primers if you intend on multiplexing.
    The NEB multiplex oligos for Illumina are the same adaptor sequence as the in-house Illumina ones

First strand cDNA synthesis...............................................................................................

Take the prepared mixture from the mRNA purification and shearing Incubate the samples as follows:

  1. 10 min at 25 C
  2. 15 min at 42 C
  3. 15 min at 70 C
  4. Hold at 4 C
It is not necessary to clean up the reaction afterwards.

2nd strand synthesis.................................................................................................................

  1. Add the following to the first strand synthesis reaction.
    2. Reagent x1 volume (uL) x6 volume + 10% (uL)
    2nd strand synthesis mix with dUTP 8 52.8
    Second strand synthesis enzyme mix 4 24.6
    Nuclease free water 48 316.4
  2. Mix by pipette.
  3. Incubate in a thermocycler for 1 hour at 16 C. The samples can be safely stored at 4 C overnight as cDNA.

dA-tail library...........................................................................................................................................................

Set the heat block to 37 C and clean up using a column

Adapter ligation of library....................................................................................................................................

This is a critical step. Perform in a PCR machine set to 25 C.
Make sure the AMpure beads have equilibrated to room temp.
Make a master mix of the adapter-reaction buffer mixture. Add the ligase separate and take out of freezer at the last minute.
Add USER enzyme after the ligation and set to 37 C
Purify by 1.8x conc AMpure beads (90uL)

Size selection....................................................................................................................................................

Another critical step. Start with 0.9x Ampure beads (135uL). Mix well
After incubating on the magnet keep the supernatant
Add 0.2x beads (30uL) to the supernatant and mix well. After incubation on the magnet Keep the beads

PCR enrich the library and attach indexes.................................................................................................................................

Use a PCR tube again. Make sure the right tube has the right index added to it!
The cycle number can be between 10 - 12 cycles depending on the input mRNA. 12 cycles is recommended for 50ng mRNA.
Purify with a PCR cleanup column

Assess the quality of the cDNA library before sequencing by running on a high sensitivity chip bioanalyser.

Some tips for when handling beads

  • When opening tube lids on the magnet, hold down the tube so liquid doesn't splash everywhere
  • If the pellet is diffuse, especially during enzymatic clean-up give the tube a jiggle when still in the magnet
  • Suck up supernatant slowly as to not disturb the beads
  • For the final elution it is critical not to contaminate the PCR with beads. It is better to elute twice
  • The volume of beads for size selection is critical. Measure the volume of DNA by pipette. It is more accurate to dial down.
    Top up the solution to 150uL if required
