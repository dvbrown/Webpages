Shearing of genomic DNA on covaris sonicator

You will need the following consumables

  • 1.1ug of high quality genomic DNA per sample
  • NEBnext DNA library preparation kit for Illumina. Only need up to adaptor ligation
    1. Illumina adaptors 1 - 12 (kit A)
  • Qiaquick PCR clean up columns or equivalent
  • 550uL Recommended, may change for size selection AMpure XP beads. Bring to room temperature before use
  • Fresh 80% ethanol for use with the beads
  • SeqCap EZ reagent kit. This contains human COT DNA, primers, PCR master mix and hybridisation/ wash buffers.
  • Dynabeads M-270 strepavidin beads for library capture
  • qPCR reagents for enrichment quantification
    1. At least 4 primer pairs for NSC control regions. Consider ordering custom regions.
  • PCR grade water

Normalise the gDNA samples to a final volume of 60 μl at 20 ng/μl (1.1ug total) into each well of the new 0.2 ml tube labeled with the DNA barcode.

  1. Gently pippette the DNA into a covaris tube, puncturing the rubber stopper. Be careful not to introduce air bubbles at this step.
  2. Use a Covaris shearer with the following settings:
    • Time: 120 seconds
    • Temperature: 7 degrees
    • Duty factor: 10%
    • Intensity: 5
    • Cycles per burst: 200
    • Water level: 12
  3. Spin down the covaris tube in a tommy centrifuge and remove the cap. Transfer liquid into a clean 0.2mL tube.
  4. Run 4.5uL of the DNA on an E-gel to inspect the size distribution.
