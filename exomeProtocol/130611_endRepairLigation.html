Procedure for the production of exomic DNA libraries for Illumina sequencing

You will need the following consumables

  • 1ug of high quality genomic DNA per sample
  • NEBnext DNA library preparation kit for Illumina. Only need up to adaptor ligation
    1. Illumina adaptors 1 - 12 (kit A)
  • Qiaquick PCR clean up columns or equivalent
  • 550uL Recommended, may change for size selection AMpure XP beads. Bring to room temperature before use
  • Fresh 80% ethanol for use with the beads
  • SeqCap EZ reagent kit. This contains human COT DNA, primers, PCR master mix and hybridisation/ wash buffers.
  • Dynabeads M-270 strepavidin beads for library capture
  • qPCR reagents for enrichment quantification
    1. At least 4 primer pairs for NSC control regions. Consider ordering custom regions.
  • PCR grade water

End repair sheared DNA..................................................................................................................................

Starting material 1ug of sheared gDNA.

  1. Prepare the following reagents Green lids:
    Component Volume (uL)
    End prep Enzyme Mix 3.0
    End Repair Reaction Buffer (10X) 6.5uL
    Fragmented DNA 55.5uL
    Total volume 65.0

  2. Pre-heat the thermal cycler to 30°C. Pre-heat the lid to 100°C.
  3. Perform the following incubations in a PCR machine:
    • 20°C for 30 minutes
    • 65°C for 30 minutes
    • 4°C hold
  4. Proceed immediately to Ligate Adapters. NEBnext ultra has no post-reaction clean-up.

Ligate adaptors................................................................................................................................................

Cleanup and size select using AmpureXP beads. Have these at room temp for 30 min prior to use

  1. Prepare the following reagents Red lids
    Component Volume (uL)
    End Prep reaction mixture 65.0
    Blunt/TA Ligase Master Mix 15.0
    NEBNext Adapter for Illumina 2.5uL
    Ligation Enhancer 1.0uL
    Total volume 83.5

  2. Incubate at 20°C for 15 minutes in a thermal cycler.
  3. Add 3μl of USER enzyme to each reaction. Mix thoroughly.
  4. Incubate at 37°C for 15 minutes in a thermal cycler.

Size selection...............................................................................................................................................

The following size selection protocol is for libraries with 200 bp inserts only.

  1. To the ligation reaction (83.5 μl), add 16.5 μl nuclease-free water to bring the reaction volume to 100 μl.
  2. Add 60 μl (0.6X) resuspended AMPure XP beads and mix well on a vortex mixer or by pipetting up and down at least 10 times.
  3. Incubate at room temperature for 5 minutes.
  4. Place the tube on an appropriate magnetic rack to separate beads from the supernatant. After the solution is clear (about 5 minutes), carefully transfer the supernatant containing your DNA to a new tube (Caution: do not discard the supernatant).
  5. Add 25 μl (0.25X) resuspended AMPure XP beads to the supernatant (160 μl), mix well and incubate for 5 minutes at room temperature.
  6. Wash 2 times with 200uL 80% ethanol.
  7. Air dry beads for 10 minutes while the tube is on the magnetic rack with the lid open.
  8. Elute DNA target from the beads with 25 μl nuclease-free water. Mix well on a vortex mixer or by pipetting up and down, then put the tube in the magnetic rack until the solution is clear.
  9. Without disturbing the bead pellet, transfer 20 μl of the supernatant to a clean PCR tube, and proceed to PCR enrichment. Contamination of beads will inhibit downstream PCR.
