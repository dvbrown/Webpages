Materials

You will need:

  • Cell dissociation reagent: Liberase

  • Scissors, forceps, razors to mince tissues. These are in the animal surgery room in a box marked "Morokoff"

Minicing and digestion of the brain tumour

The majority of the protocol is from Venugopal et al "Processing of Primary Brain Tumor Tissue for Stem Cell Assays and Flow Sorting" 2012 Jove Processing of Primary Brain Tumor Tissue with some alterations. Patchinson et al "Optimized flow cytometric analysis of central nervous system tissue reveals novel functional relationships among cells expressing CD133, CD15, and CD24." Stem Cells was also consulted Patchinson et al

  1. Add 200 μl thawed Liberase (200 μg/mL = 0.62 WU/mL) (Roche Applied Science) to 15 ml of 1x HBSS and place into 37 °C water bath. Unlike Trypsin-EDTA, the Liberase method preserves the surface antigen CD133. For a tissue sample of about 0.5 cm3, we use 200 μl of Liberase. If the tissue is smaller, we use 100 ul.
  2. Bring ammonium chloride solution (Stem cell technologies) to room temperature. Ammonium Chloride solution gently lyses red blood cells with minimal effect on other cells. It does not contain a fixative.
  3. In a sterile biological safety cabinet, add 5 ml of aCSF to specimen container, swirl to rinse tissue, then pipette off. This step helps to remove red blood cells (RBC).
  4. Transfer brain tumor tissue to a sterile 100 mm Petri dish. Gently mince human tumor tissue with a scalpel to a slurry consistency.
  5. Collect sample using a 10 ml regular pipette or forceps and transfer fragments into the tube containing pre-warmed aCSF with Liberase. Place in 37 °C waterbath, for 30 - 90min, mixing every 5 - 10 min with a p1000 pipette.
    After each tituration wait 5min for the tissue to settle and take the top 1mL of solution (floating cells) to avoid disturbing them store these cells on ice to preserve viability.
  6. For preparations with poor viability treat cells for 15 to 30 minutes in a solution of 100 µg/mL DNAse and 5 mM MgCl2 in HBSS at room temperature. The FACS buffer cannot contain EDTA in this instance.
  7. Filter the tissue lysate through 70 μm cell strainer into a 50 ml Falcon tube. Spin the filtrate down at 300 x g for 5 min. The percoll can be washed by resuspending in HBSS and spinning 200 × g for 5 min
  8. After accutase dissociation, resuspend cells in 30% Stock Isotonic Percoll (SIP) solution/ 70% 1x HBSS and centrifuged for 10 minutes at 700 g at room temperature.
    This protocol was obtained from Nikodemova et al 2012; J Neuroinflammation. Use plastic pipettes as percoll adheres to glass.
  9. Remove supernatant carefully and evaluate size and colour of the resulting cell pellet: pellets which are pink or red indicate increasing numbers of red blood cells.
    Resuspend pellet in 1 ml PBS. Add an appropriate amount of ammonium chloride solution (4-12 ml) based on pellet size and red cell contamination (the ammonium chloride solution is very gentle and increased amounts are not harmful to cells other than red cells).
    10. Incubate at room temperature for 5 min. Spin cells down at 300 x g for 5 min and wash once with 10 ml of FACS buffer
  10. Proceed to FACS staining.

Myelin removal by percoll

In order to use Percoll to prepare a gradient, the osmolality of Percoll (undiluted) must first be adjusted with saline or cell culture medium to make Percoll isotonic with physiological salt solutions. Adding 9 parts (v/v) of Percoll to 1 part 10× HBSS is a simple way of preparing a Stock Isotonic Percoll (SIP) solution.

Antibody labelling for FACS

  1. Spin down at 500xg at 4°C for 5 minutes.
  2. Resuspend in 90uL for 100uL final vol
  3. Add 5uL of CD133-APC and 5uL of CD44-FITC to samples. For the control samples, add 5uL of isotype-APC.
  4. Be sure to run the PI only, CD133-APC only and CD44-FITC only controls and ensure proper compensation.
    5. Incubate for 20 minutes in the dark in the fridge (dark).
  5. Wash with 1mL of FACS buffer and centrifuge for 500xg at 4°C for 5 minutes.
  6. Resuspend in a sufficient amount minimum 500uL of FACS buffer for FACS sorting. Aim for 5 - 10 million cells per mL.
  7. Before sorting add 1:100 of 1mg/mL (final 1ug/mL) PI to each tube. This is 5uL of Iwan's buffer for 500uL cells.
  8. Collect 5 different populations {(CD133+, CD44+). (CD133+, CD44-), (CD133-, CD44+), (CD133-, CD44-)} and mixed population.

Fluorescence compensation is the mathematical subtraction of the fluorescence due to one fluorochrome from the fluorescence due to another fluorochrome. This is necessary because emission spectra from two fluorochromes can overlap.

Appendix A: recipe for artificial CSF (aCSF)

From Cold Spring Harbour protocols CSH recipe

  1. 119 mM NaCl - 58.44 g/mol
  2. 26.2 mM NaHCO3 - 84.007 g/mol
  3. 2.5 mm KCl - 74.5513 g/mol
  4. 1 mM NaH2PO4 - 119.98 g/mol
  5. 1.3 mM MgCl2 - 95.211 g/mol
  6. 10 mM glucose - 180.1559 g/mol

Gas with 5% CO2/95% O2 for 10–15 min, then add 2.5-mm CaCl2. Filter sterilize with a 0.22-µm filter apparatus, and store at 4°C. ACSF is stable for 3–4 wk. If overt contamination (solution becomes cloudy) or precipitation is apparent, discard, and make fresh ACSF.

Consider the use of 200 U/mL DNase in the dissociation mixture if DNA precipitation is an issue.

Appendix E: Preparation of liberase

Info below from the Roche website Liberase. Purified collagenase contains approximately 1 mole of zinc and 2 to 7 moles of calcium per mole of enzyme (3). Exposure of the enzyme to divalent cation chelators removes zinc and calcium, thus rendering the enzyme inactive.

  1. Reconstitute the lyophilized enzyme with injection-quality sterile water . Do not add serum or other components that may influence enzyme activity (e.g., albumin or protease inhibitors) to the dissociation buffer
  2. Reconstituting in 2mL for a small vial will result in a concentration of 13 Wünsch units/ml or total collagenase concentration of 2.5mg/mL
  3. Place vial on ice to rehydrate the lyophilized enzyme.
  4. Gently agitate the vial at +2 to + 8°C until enzyme is completely dissolved (max. 30 min).
  5. Remove an aliquot of the stock solution to prepare the working solution.
  6. Store unused stock solution in single-use aliquots at –20°C.
