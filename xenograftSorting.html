Materials

You will need:

  • HBSS media. Some people use artificial CSF solution.
  • Cell dissociation reagent: one of collagenase, liberase (collagenase blend), accutase or papain. I can't find much consistency in the literature.
  • RBC lysis kit or set of buffers to lyse erytrocytes.
  • Scissors, forceps, razors to mince tissues
  • Flame polished glass pasteur pipettes

Procedure

Maintaining viability is essential to get a reasonable number of cells for experiments at the end. Especially when considering the stem cell fraction will be low

Appendix A: recipe for artificial CSF (aCSF)

From Cold Spring Harbour protocols CSH recipe

  1. 119 mM NaCl - 58.44 g/mol
  2. 26.2 mM NaHCO3 - 84.007 g/mol
  3. 2.5 mm KCl - 74.5513 g/mol
  4. 1 mM NaH2PO4 - 119.98 g/mol
  5. 1.3 mM MgCl2 - 95.211 g/mol
  6. 10 mM glucose - 180.1559 g/mol

Gas with 5% CO2/95% O2 for 10–15 min, then add 2.5-mm CaCl2. Filter sterilize with a 0.22-µm filter apparatus, and store at 4°C. ACSF is stable for 3–4 wk. If overt contamination (solution becomes cloudy) or precipitation is apparent, discard, and make fresh ACSF.
