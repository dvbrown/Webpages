Materials

You will need:

  • HBSS media. Some people use artificial CSF solution.
  • Cell dissociation reagent: one of collagenase, liberase (collagenase blend), accutase or papain. I can't find much consistency in the literature.
  • Ammonium chloride solution to lyse erytrocytes.
  • Scissors, forceps, razors to mince tissues
  • Liquid nitrogen in an esky for snap and OCT freezing
  • 4% PFA for FFPE fixation

Procedure

Maintaining viability is essential to get a reasonable number of cells for experiments at the end. Especially when considering the stem cell fraction will be low

Minicing and digestion of the brain tumour

The majority of the protocol is from Venugopal et al "Processing of Primary Brain Tumor Tissue for Stem Cell Assays and Flow Sorting" 2012 Jove Processing of Primary Brain Tumor Tissue with some alterations. Patchinson et al "Optimized flow cytometric analysis of central nervous system tissue reveals novel functional relationships among cells expressing CD133, CD15, and CD24." Stem Cells was also consultedPatchinson et al

  1. Add 200 μl thawed Liberase (200 μg/mL = 0.62 WU/mL) (Roche Applied Science) to 15 ml of 1x HBSS and place into 37 °C water bath. Unlike Trypsin-EDTA, the Liberase method preserves the surface antigen CD133. For a tissue sample of about 0.5 cm3, we use 200 μl of Liberase. If the tissue is smaller, we use 100 ul.
  2. Bring ammonium chloride solution (Stem cell technologies) to room temperature. Ammonium Chloride solution gently lyses red blood cells with minimal effect on other cells. It does not contain a fixative.
  3. In a sterile biological safety cabinet, add 5 ml of aCSF to specimen container, swirl to rinse tissue, then pipette off. This step helps to remove red blood cells (RBC).
  4. Transfer brain tumor tissue to a sterile 100 mm Petri dish. Gently mince human tumor tissue with a scalpel to a slurry consistency.
  5. Collect sample using a 10 ml regular pipette or forceps and transfer fragments into the tube containing pre-warmed aCSF with Liberase. Place in 37 °C waterbath, for 30 - 90min, mixing every 5 - 10 min with a p1000 pipette.
    After each tituration wait 5min for the tissue to settle and take the top 1mL of solution (floating cells) to avoid disturbing them.
  6. Filter the tissue lysate through 70 μm cell strainer into a 50 ml Falcon tube. Spin the filtrate down at 300 x g for 5 min.
  7. Remove supernatant carefully and evaluate size and colour of the resulting cell pellet: pellets which are pink or red indicate increasing numbers of red blood cells. Resuspend pellet in 1 ml PBS. Add an appropriate amount of ammonium chloride solution (4-12 ml) based on pellet size and red cell contamination (the ammonium chloride solution is very gentle and increased amounts are not harmful to cells other than red cells).
    8. Incubate at room temperature for 5 min. Spin cells down at 300 x g for 5 min and wash once with 10 ml of FACS buffer
  8. Proceed to FACS staining.

Appendix A: recipe for artificial CSF (aCSF)

From Cold Spring Harbour protocols CSH recipe

  1. 119 mM NaCl - 58.44 g/mol
  2. 26.2 mM NaHCO3 - 84.007 g/mol
  3. 2.5 mm KCl - 74.5513 g/mol
  4. 1 mM NaH2PO4 - 119.98 g/mol
  5. 1.3 mM MgCl2 - 95.211 g/mol
  6. 10 mM glucose - 180.1559 g/mol

Gas with 5% CO2/95% O2 for 10–15 min, then add 2.5-mm CaCl2. Filter sterilize with a 0.22-µm filter apparatus, and store at 4°C. ACSF is stable for 3–4 wk. If overt contamination (solution becomes cloudy) or precipitation is apparent, discard, and make fresh ACSF.

Consider the use of 200 U/mL DNase in the dissociation mixture if DNA precipitation is an issue.

Appendix E: Preparation of liberase

Info below from the Roche website Liberase. Purified collagenase contains approximately 1 mole of zinc and 2 to 7 moles of calcium per mole of enzyme (3). Exposure of the enzyme to divalent cation chelators removes zinc and calcium, thus rendering the enzyme inactive.

  1. Reconstitute the lyophilized enzyme with injection-quality sterile water . Do not add serum or other components that may influence enzyme activity (e.g., albumin or protease inhibitors) to the dissociation buffer
  2. Reconstituting in 2mL for a small vial will result in a concentration of 13 Wünsch units/ml or total collagenase concentration of 2.5mg/mL
  3. Place vial on ice to rehydrate the lyophilized enzyme.
  4. Gently agitate the vial at +2 to + 8°C until enzyme is completely dissolved (max. 30 min).
  5. Remove an aliquot of the stock solution to prepare the working solution.
  6. Store unused stock solution in single-use aliquots at –20°C.
