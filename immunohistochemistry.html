Immunohistochemistry staining protocol

The traditional DAB/ brown staining for tissue slides.

Materials

  • 10x PBS-tween to make 1x PBST
  • Xylene. Gulay recommends fresh
  • Ethanol. Gulay recommends fresh
  • Antigen retrieval buffer (citrate A and B)
  • Microwave
  • 30% hydrogen peroxide (fridge)
  • Hydrophobic (pap) pen
  • Goat serum and BSA (freezer and fridge respectively)
  • Haemotox in the chemical shelf.

Day 1:

  1. Rehydrate the slides.
    1. Xylene x2 for 5 min each
    2. 100, 100, 80, 60, 30% ethanol for 2 min each.
    3. Wash in running tap water for 2 min.
  2. Antigen retrieval
    1. Mix 4.5mL solution A with 20.5mL solution B to 250mL with RO water.
    2. Preheat this buffer in the microwave for about 5min.
    3. Place buffer in a glass container on a hotplate set for 90 C until boiling.
    4. Boil the slides for 20min
    5. Take the slides off the boiler and cool to room temp. You may need to top up the glass container with water if it has evaporated.
  3. Wash in distilled water
  4. Block peroxidases.
    1. Make up approx 200 - 250mL 3% hydrogen peroxide in a glass container by diluting the 30% stock. Incubate for 10 minat room temp.
    2. Wash off the peroxide with distilled water.
  5. Draw a circle around each section with a pap pen. Be mindful of not letting the slides dry out.
  6. Block slides
    1. Make up a blocking buffer using a solution with 2% serum and 0.1% BSA
    2. You may need to filter this if there is precipitant.
    3. Incubate 30 minutes at room temp. Afterwards tap off liquid, DO NOT rinse.
  7. Primary antibody
    1. Dilute your antibody to the required concentration using blocking buffer. 50 - 75uL should be sufficient per slide.
    2. Drop enough onto the slide to cover it and place in a humidified chamber. Get a lunchbox and wet some tissues. Close the lunch box
    3. Store in a cool room overnight.

Day 2 protocol

  1. Wash the slides once in water then twice in PBST for 2 min each
  2. Secondary antibody
    1. Dilute out the to a standard concentration of 1:500 in PBST.
    2. 50 - 75uL per slide should be sufficient. Incubate for 30 minutes at room temp.
    3. Wash the slides once in water then twice in PBST for 2 min each
  3. Strepavidin incubation
    1. Prepare vector stain well in advance by mixing 10mL PBST with 2 drops solution A then 2 drops solution B. Incubate for 30 minutes at room temp to activate.
    2. Add vector stain to slides and incubate 20 min at room temp.
    3. Wash the slides once in water then twice in PBST for 2 min each
  4. DAB incubation/ colour development Critical step
    1. Prepare the DAB solution. Don't add nickel
    2. Add the substrate solution for 2 - 20 minutes depending on the intensity required. You will be required to sit and watch it.
  5. Counter stain
    1. Dilute out the Haematox 1:20 with water.
    2. Add haematoxylin for 1 - 5 seconds depending on intensity wanted.
    3. Add Scott's tap water directly after haematoxylin for approx 30 seconds.
    4. Dunk the slides in water. Run water for 2 minutes.
  6. Dehydrating
    1. 30, 60, 80, 100, 100% ethanol for 2 min each.
    2. Xylene x2 for 5 min each
  7. Coverslip the samples with DPX.
